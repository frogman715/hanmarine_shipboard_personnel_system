## 1. ALL USER ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 1.1 SHORE-BASED ROLES

#### **DIRECTOR**
- **Job Description:**
  - Overall in-charge of the entire affairs of the company in all aspects
  - Adviser with overriding authority for company affairs
  - Final approver for all major decisions
  
- **Permissions & Actions:**
  - Approve Quality Management System (HGQS)
  - Approve recruitment of personnel
  - Approve external providers/subcontractors
  - Final approval on management decisions
  - Sign contracts and agreements

---

#### **QUALITY MANAGEMENT REPRESENTATIVE (QMR) / CREWING MANAGER**
- **Job Description:**
  - Overseer and responsible for effective implementation of Quality Management System
  - In-charge of communication to principals (ship owners)
  - Conducts interviews for maritime employee applicants
  - Plans staff and supervises all assigned work units

- **Responsibilities:**
  - Ensure officers and ratings comply with STCW/FLAG STATE/CLIENT requirements
  - Handle all legal matters concerning officers and crew
  - Communicate with principals regarding flight schedules and requirements
  - Responsible for CBA (Collective Bargaining Agreement) matters
  - Administer assessments to maritime applicants
  - Select qualified and competent crew to customers' requirements
  - Arrange line-up of assigned vessels
  - Organize stand-by crew
  - Recruit potential applicants outside main office
  - Responsible for booking flights
  - Overall in-charge of training for officers and crew
  - Responsible for cadetship program
  - Monitor HGQS effectiveness and report to management
  
- **Permissions:**
  - Approve crew selection and deployment
  - Approve training programs
  - Access to all crew records
  - Communication authority with principals/owners
  - Approve document processing for crew

---

#### **EXPERT STAFF**
- **Qualifications Required:**
  - Good communication skills in English (Active)
  - Competency certificate equivalent to Level III Nautical Expert (ANT III) or Level III Technician (ATT III) or Diploma IV
  - Minimum 5 years experience in passenger ships OR 3 years experience in foreign shipping/vessels
  - Understanding of MLC 2006 Reg. 1.2
  - Good attitude and health
  
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Expert consultation on maritime matters
  - Technical guidance for crew selection
  - Advisory role in decision making

---

#### **CREWING DEPARTMENT**
- **Responsibilities:**
  - Set standards for applicants
  - Ensure applicants are given due attention
  - Professional approach in candidate screening
  - Detect falsified documents/certificates/visas
  - Avoid extended waiting time for applicants
  - Carefully check encoded data before sending
  - Ensure effective and comprehensive training
  - Maintain training records
  - Use approved shore-based training facilities
  - Encourage seafarers to improve qualifications

- **What They Can Detect/Prevent:**
  - Falsified documents
  - Falsified certificates
  - Fraudulent visas
  - Fraudulent entries in SIRB (Seafarer's Identification Record Book)
  - Altered stamps

---

#### **DOCUMENTATION OFFICER / ADMIN STAFF**
- **Qualifications:**
  - Communication skills in English
  - Minimum Senior High School education
  - Operating MS Office proficiency
  - Understanding of MLC 2006 Reg. 1.2
  - Understanding of document requirements for seafarers

## 2. COMPLETE CREW MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW

### **PHASE 1: RECRUITMENT & APPLICATION**

#### **Step 1: Initial Contact**
- Applicant logs in name and contact number
- Given appointment slip
- Informed of dress code

#### **Step 2: Application Form**
- Fill up Application for Employment (HGF-CR-02)
- Submit valid documents and certificates

#### **Step 3: Document Verification**
- **Documentation Officer receives documents**
- **Verification Methods:**
  - Physical checking (paper quality, signatures, hologram)
  - Online verification via SEACOM (Departemen Perhubungan Laut)
  - Barcode checking
  - Money detector for authenticity
  
- **Documents Required:**
  - Passport (minimum 15 months validity)
  - Seaman Book (SIRB) (minimum 15 months validity)
  - Certificates of Competency (COC)
  - Certificates of Proficiency (COP)
  - Flag State Endorsements
  - Basic Safety Training certificates
  - Medical Certificate (valid max 2 years)
  - Color Vision Certificate (valid max 6 years)
  - Training certificates (STCW compliant)
  
- **Issue Receiving Checklist:**
  - Form printed and copied
  - Original filed, copy given to applicant
  - Expired documents flagged for revalidation

#### **Step 4: Interview with Crewing Manager**
- Crewing Manager conducts interview
- Check authenticity of documents
- Character check if doubtful entries
- Advise applicant of company standards
- Carefully check application form entries

#### **Step 5: Assessment (if needed)**
- Endorsed to Crewing Department for assessment
- Passing score: 75%
- Below 75%: May be reconsidered with special briefing/training

#### **Step 6: Final Interview with Director**
- Applicant interviewed by Operations Manager
- Final interview with QMR and Director
- Final decision made

---

### **PHASE 2: APPROVAL PROCESS**

#### **Step 7: Principal/Owner Approval**
- Documentation Officer scans all certificates
- Create folder with crew name in scanning system
- Input data to crew database (certificate numbers, issue dates, expiry dates)
- Create CV according to flag state requirements
- Send CV to ship owner/principal for approval

**Approval Decision:**
- **IF REJECTED:** CV filed for future promotion to other owners
- **IF APPROVED:** Proceed to Step 8

---

### **PHASE 3: PRE-DEPLOYMENT PROCESSING**

#### **Step 8: Documentation Department Processing**
- Receive approval from principal
- Create Document Checklist including:
  1. Documents Checklist (HGF-CR-01)
  2. Next of Kin form
  3. Declaration forms
  4. Training Record
  5. Checklist for Departing Crew (HGF-CR-03)
  
- **Process Visa Requirements:**
  - Apply for necessary visas based on vessel flag/route
  - Ensure visa validity meets contract duration

- **Encode identification numbers:**
  - Enter into system
  - Send to principal for final confirmation

#### **Step 9: Accounting Department**
- Applicant presents receiving checklist
- Fill up Allotment Information Sheet
- Issue bank referral for preferred bank
- Open allotment account
- Explain payment terms:
  - Monthly payment schedule
  - Allotment to family arrangements
  - Currency exchange rates
  - Final wage calculation procedures

#### **Step 10: Contract Signing**
- Sign Contract of Employment (HGF-CR-10)
- Review terms and conditions
- Ensure seafarer understands:
  - Rights and duties under employment agreement
  - Wage rates and payment schedule
  - Working hours and rest periods
  - Leave entitlements
  - Repatriation rights
  - Complaint procedures
- Witness signatures (KPI Union representative, DGST representative if required)
- Provide copy to seafarer

---

### **PHASE 4: TRAINING**

#### **Step 11: In-House Training**
- Crewing submits names to Training Department
- Contact candidates by phone to confirm attendance
- **Training Topics Include:**
  - Customer's specific requirements
  - Ship-specific procedures
  - Safety management system
  - Company policies
  - MLC 2006 compliance
  - Complaint procedures
  - Emergency procedures
  
- **Emergency Joining:** Special briefing with endorsement from Crewing Manager, approved by Operations Manager

- **Training Assessment:**
  - Assessment during training
  - Passing score required
  - Certificate issued upon completion
  - Records maintained in crew file

---

### **PHASE 5: FINAL DISPATCH**

#### **Step 12: Pre-Departure**
- Update Crew List
- Update Crew Replacement Plan (CRP)
- Update HUBLA (Sea Transportation Authority) records
- Arrange flight booking
- Provide crew with:
  - Flight itinerary
  - Contact numbers
  - Emergency contact list
  - Ship joining instructions
  - Port agent details

#### **Step 13: Departure & Joining Vessel**
- Issue Notice of Crew On-Signing (HGF-CR-17)
- Crew dispatched to vessel
- Company representative may accompany to airport
- Provide emergency contact numbers

---

### **PHASE 6: ON-BOARD PERIOD**

#### **Crew Status: ONBOARD**
- Monthly wage payments processed
- Allotments sent to families
- Regular communication with vessel
- Monitor certificate expiry dates
- Respond to crew complaints/issues
- Provide support for medical situations
- Coordinate with Master for crew welfare

#### **Continuous Monitoring:**
- Medical issues on board
- Disciplinary issues
- Performance appraisal
- Training needs
- Certificate renewals
- Contract extension discussions

---

### **PHASE 7: SIGN-OFF / DISEMBARKATION**

#### **Step 1: Disembarkation Notice**
- Receive information from principal/vessel
- Prepare Disembarkation Application (HGF-CR-13)
- Arrange relief crew

#### **Step 2: Sign-off Process**
- Issue Notice of Crew Off-Signing (HGF-CR-17)
- Arrange repatriation
- Process travel documents

#### **Step 3: Arrival & Reporting**
- Crew reports to Crewing Department
- Submit passport and Seaman Book
- Fill up De-briefing Form (HGF-CR-04)
- Interview with Crewing Manager

#### **Step 4: Final Settlement**
- Passport and Seaman Book endorsed to Accounting
- Calculate final wages based on sign-off date
- Process final payment
- Issue Statement of Account (HGF-AC-05)

#### **Step 5: Document Return**
- Sign Documents Checklist as receipt
- Withdraw personal documents if desired
- File maintained for future deployment

## 3. CREW STATUS LIFECYCLE

### **Primary Statuses:**

1. **APPLICANT**
   - Initial application submitted
   - Under review/interview process
   - Awaiting approval

2. **APPROVED**
   - Passed all interviews and assessments
   - Documents verified
   - Ready for principal approval

3. **STANDBY**
   - Approved by principal
   - Completed all pre-deployment requirements
   - Training completed
   - Awaiting vessel assignment
   - Available for immediate deployment

4. **ONBOARD**
   - Actively serving on vessel
   - Employment contract in effect
   - Receiving wages
   - Under vessel's operational command

5. **SIGN-OFF / DISEMBARKING**
   - Completing current contract
   - In process of leaving vessel
   - Repatriation arranged

6. **VACATION / OFF-DUTY**
   - Completed contract
   - On shore leave
   - May be available for re-hire
   - Maintaining relationship with company

7. **EX-CREW**
   - Previously worked for company
   - Available for re-hiring
   - Good appraisal: Priority for re-deployment
   - Low appraisal: Require retraining before re-deployment

8. **BLACKLISTED / TERMINATED**
   - Poor performance/conduct
   - Fraudulent documents
   - Safety violations
   - Not eligible for re-hire

---

### **Status Change Triggers:**

| From Status | To Status | Who Can Change | Trigger Event |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| APPLICANT | APPROVED | Crewing Manager | Passes interview & assessment |
| APPLICANT | REJECTED | Crewing Manager/QMR | Fails assessment/verification |
| APPROVED | STANDBY | QMR | Principal approval received |
| STANDBY | ONBOARD | Crewing Manager | Joins vessel, Notice signed-on |
| ONBOARD | SIGN-OFF | Crewing Manager | Contract completion/early termination |
| SIGN-OFF | VACATION | Crewing Manager | Completed de-briefing |
| VACATION | STANDBY | Crewing Manager | Available for re-deployment |
| VACATION | EX-CREW | System/Crewing Mgr | Extended time without deployment |
| ONBOARD | MEDICAL-OFF | Crewing Manager | Medical emergency |
| ONBOARD | TERMINATED | QMR/Director | Serious misconduct |
| EX-CREW | APPLICANT | Self | Applies for new position |

---

### **Who Can Change Status:**

- **Crewing Manager:** Day-to-day status changes (Standby ‚Üî Onboard ‚Üî Sign-off)
- **QMR:** Major status changes (Approved, Terminated)
- **Director:** Final authority on termination/blacklisting
- **System Auto:** Time-based status changes (Vacation ‚Üí Ex-crew after extended period)

### **6.2 MEDICAL CHECKLIST (Reg 1.2 MLC 2006)**

**Medical Certificate Requirements:**
- [ ] Issued by licensed medical practitioner
- [ ] Written in English
- [ ] Valid for maximum 2 years
- [ ] Contains seafarer photo and signature
- [ ] States position/capacity on ship

**Medical Examination Items:**
- [ ] Hearing: Satisfactory without restriction
- [ ] Sight: Satisfactory without restriction
- [ ] Color Vision: Satisfactory (for positions requiring it)
  - [ ] Color Vision Certificate valid max 6 years
- [ ] Not suffering from conditions that:
  - [ ] Would be aggravated by service at sea
  - [ ] Would render seafarer unfit for service
  - [ ] Would endanger health of others on board

**Specific Medical Tests:**
- [ ] Blood pressure check
- [ ] Vision test
- [ ] Hearing test
- [ ] Color blindness test (if position requires)
- [ ] Chest X-ray
- [ ] Blood tests
- [ ] Urine tests
- [ ] Drug screening
- [ ] Alcohol screening
- [ ] Physical fitness assessment
- [ ] Mental health assessment

**Medical Certificate Follow-up:**
- [ ] Copy filed in seafarer's records
- [ ] Expiry date monitored
- [ ] Renewal scheduled before expiration
- [ ] Principal provided with copy

**Unfit for Sea Duty:**
- [ ] If declared unfit: MUST NOT be employed
- [ ] Must be declared fit by same licensed practitioner
- [ ] Re-examination arranged if needed
- [ ] Alternative medical opinion sought if disputed

**Who Verifies:** Documentation Officer  
**Medical Authority:** Licensed Medical Practitioner  
**Final Approval:** Crewing Manager
